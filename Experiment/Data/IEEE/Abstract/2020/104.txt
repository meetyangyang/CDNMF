This paper presents a liveness analysis method for sequential automated manufacturing systems (AMSs);  which can be modeled by a class of Petri nets named systems of sequential systems with shared resources (S\n<sup xmlns:mml=\http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML\ xmlns:xlink=\http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink\>4</sup>\nPR). We show that deadlocks in S\n<sup xmlns:mml=\http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML\ xmlns:xlink=\http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink\>4</sup>\nPR can be characterized by the saturation of its structural object named a perfect activity circuit (PA-circuits). Thus;  S\n<sup xmlns:mml=\http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML\ xmlns:xlink=\http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink\>4</sup>\nPR is live if and only if no PA-circuits in it is saturated at all reachable states. A PA-circuits of an S\n<sup xmlns:mml=\http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML\ xmlns:xlink=\http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink\>4</sup>\nPR may not be saturated at any state; hence;  we propose an integer linear program (ILP) to determine whether a PA-circuits can be saturated or not. Then an algorithm is proposed to compute the set of PA-circuits that may be saturated. This presented method nontrivially generalizes deadlock characterization and liveness condition of ordinary Petri nets to a broader class of nonordinary ones.
