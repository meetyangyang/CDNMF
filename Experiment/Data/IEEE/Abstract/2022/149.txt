The rapid development of sensing technologies has enabled sensors embedded into systems and generated continuous online data for anomaly monitoring and protection of the system. However;  due to transmission errors and environmental noises;  the online measurements collected by sensors are usually inaccurate and cannot be applied directly. A small amount of precise \n<italic xmlns:mml=\http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML\ xmlns:xlink=\http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink\>in situ</i>\n measurements;  which are accurate but costly to acquire;  are usually fused with online measurements for accuracy improvement. Over the past years;  there are many studies concerning such data fusion approaches for obtaining reliable fused data. However;  most existing works assume the availability of \n<italic xmlns:mml=\http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML\ xmlns:xlink=\http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink\>in situ</i>\n measurements;  without providing a strategy for sampling the expensive \n<italic xmlns:mml=\http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML\ xmlns:xlink=\http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink\>in situ</i>\n measurements. As a result;  they may undersample or oversample the \n<italic xmlns:mml=\http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML\ xmlns:xlink=\http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink\>in situ</i>\n measurements that result in large fusion errors or unnecessary costs. To address this problem;  this article proposes a dynamic policy for sampling the \n<italic xmlns:mml=\http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML\ xmlns:xlink=\http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink\>in situ</i>\n measurements adaptively using the information that the online measurements provide. Specifically;  the proposed policy models the dependence between \n<italic xmlns:mml=\http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML\ xmlns:xlink=\http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink\>in situ</i>\n sampling decisions and influencing factors using a policy network;  predicts the probability of recommending \n<italic xmlns:mml=\http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML\ xmlns:xlink=\http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink\>in situ</i>\n samplings with the policy network;  and guides the sampling of \n<italic xmlns:mml=\http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML\ xmlns:xlink=\http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink\>in situ</i>\n measurements dynamically.
