In practical systems;  due to reasons such as sensor limitations;  sensor faults;  and packet losses in networks;  the observation of events becomes nondeterministic. In this article;  we extend strong detectability and weak detectability to the case of nondeterministic observations and denote them as A-(\n<inline-formula xmlns:mml=\http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML\ xmlns:xlink=\http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink\> <tex-math notation=\LaTeX\>$k_{1}$ </tex-math></inline-formula>\n;  \n<inline-formula xmlns:mml=\http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML\ xmlns:xlink=\http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink\> <tex-math notation=\LaTeX\>$k_{2}$ </tex-math></inline-formula>\n)-detectability and O-(\n<inline-formula xmlns:mml=\http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML\ xmlns:xlink=\http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink\> <tex-math notation=\LaTeX\>$k_{1}$ </tex-math></inline-formula>\n;  \n<inline-formula xmlns:mml=\http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML\ xmlns:xlink=\http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink\> <tex-math notation=\LaTeX\>$k_{2}$ </tex-math></inline-formula>\n)-detectability;  respectively. A-(\n<inline-formula xmlns:mml=\http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML\ xmlns:xlink=\http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink\> <tex-math notation=\LaTeX\>$k_{1}$ </tex-math></inline-formula>\n;  \n<inline-formula xmlns:mml=\http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML\ xmlns:xlink=\http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink\> <tex-math notation=\LaTeX\>$k_{2}$ </tex-math></inline-formula>\n)-detectability says that;  for any string;  we can distinguish state pairs in the specification for all possible observations of the string. O-(\n<inline-formula xmlns:mml=\http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML\ xmlns:xlink=\http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink\> <tex-math notation=\LaTeX\>$k_{1}$ </tex-math></inline-formula>\n;  \n<inline-formula xmlns:mml=\http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML\ xmlns:xlink=\http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink\> <tex-math notation=\LaTeX\>$k_{2}$ </tex-math></inline-formula>\n)-detectability says that;  for at least one string;  we can distinguish state pairs in the specification for all possible observations of the string. For A-(\n<inline-formula xmlns:mml=\http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML\ xmlns:xlink=\http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink\> <tex-math notation=\LaTeX\>$k_{1}$ </tex-math></inline-formula>\n;  \n<inline-formula xmlns:mml=\http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML\ xmlns:xlink=\http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink\> <tex-math notation=\LaTeX\>$k_{2}$ </tex-math></inline-formula>\n)-detectability;  we construct a transformed automaton and then translate the A-(\n<inline-formula xmlns:mml=\http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML\ xmlns:xlink=\http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink\> <tex-math notation=\LaTeX\>$k_{1}$ </tex-math></inline-formula>\n;  \n<inline-formula xmlns:mml=\http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML\ xmlns:xlink=\http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink\> <tex-math notation=\LaTeX\>$k_{2}$ </tex-math></inline-formula>\n)-detectability problem into the traditional detectability problem that has been solved. We show that A-(\n<inline-formula xmlns:mml=\http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML\ xmlns:xlink=\http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink\> <tex-math notation=\LaTeX\>$k_{1}$ </tex-math></inline-formula>\n;  \n<inline-formula xmlns:mml=\http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML\ xmlns:xlink=\http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink\> <tex-math notation=\LaTeX\>$k_{2}$ </tex-math></inline-formula>\n)-detectability can be used to solve the deterministic supervisory control problem. For O-(\n<inline-formula xmlns:mml=\http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML\ xmlns:xlink=\http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink\> <tex-math notation=\LaTeX\>$k_{1}$ </tex-math></inline-formula>\n;  \n<inline-formula xmlns:mml=\http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML\ xmlns:xlink=\http://www.w3.org/1999/xlink\> <tex-math notation=\LaTeX\>$k_{2}$ </tex-math></inline-formula>\n)-detectability;  we construct an augmented automaton that includes all the information of the given automaton and its state estimates.
